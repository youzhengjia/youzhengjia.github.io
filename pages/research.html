When the Cloud Comes to Town: The Environmental and Welfare Impact of Data Centers

Abstract: This paper estimates the causal effect of data center entry on local air pollution. Data centers must test backup diesel generators after receiving air permits, and that testing emits SO2. I combine facility-level permit data with NASA satellite-based grid-level SO2 measurements from 2005--2024 and estimate the effect of data center entry using a staggered difference-in-differences design. In the first year after permit issuance, SO2 rises by 0.046 DU, or 0.21 standard deviations. Using QCEW data, I also find suggestive evidence of an 8 dollar increase in average weekly wages at the county level, about 1 percent. A back-of-the-envelope welfare calculation implies a null or modest loss in the first year, only considering mortality risk from SO2 increase, while the wage bill keeps rising in later years.
